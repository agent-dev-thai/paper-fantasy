## Design Context

### Users
Solo mobile players killing time offline, especially during travel or short idle sessions. The game should feel playable one-handed and readable on a portrait phone.

### Brand Personality
Handmade, mischievous, nostalgic. Paper Fantasy should feel like a secret notebook RPG drawn during class, but polished enough that the hero, monsters, and title art have real manga cover energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light notebook-paper canvas with graphite, red teacher-mark, and blue ballpoint accents. Runtime raster art should read as polished manga ink: confident contour lines, readable fantasy silhouettes, controlled cel shading, hatching, screentone texture, and restrained paper grain. Avoid casual scratch energy for monsters, hero, props, and title art.

### Design Principles
- Keep the notebook illusion first: art must feel drawn on paper, not like detached fantasy UI.
- Prefer expressive, readable silhouettes over detail that collapses at mobile sprite size.
- Use red and blue accents sparingly so they feel like pen marks, not a full-color palette.
- Preserve offline canvas simplicity and procedural fallbacks while improving raster art quality.
